Manchester United is a professional football club based in Manchester, England. Founded in 1878, the club competes in the Premier League and has a rich history, with numerous domestic and international titles. Fans can follow their latest news, fixtures, and highlights on the official Manchester United website and app, as well as ESPN for live scores and updates.

Domestically, Manchester United have won a record 20 top-flight league titles, 13 FA Cups, 6 League Cups and a record 21 FA Community Shields. Additionally, in international football, they have won the European Cup/UEFA Champions League three times, and the UEFA Europa League, the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup, the UEFA Super Cup, the Intercontinental Cup and the FIFA Club World Cup once each.[6][7] Appointed as manager in 1945, Matt Busby built a team with an average age of just 22 nicknamed the Busby Babes that won successive league titles in the 1950s and became the first English club to compete in the European Cup.

Eight players were killed in the Munich air disaster, but Busby rebuilt the team around star players George Best, Denis Law and Bobby Charlton – known as the United Trinity. They won two more league titles before becoming the first English club to win the European Cup in 1968. After Busby's retirement, Manchester United were unable to produce sustained success until the arrival of Alex Ferguson, who became the club's longest-serving and most successful manager, winning 38 trophies including 13 league titles, five FA Cups and two Champions League titles between 1986 and 2013.[8]

In the 1998–99 season, under Ferguson, the club became the first in the history of English football to achieve the continental treble of the Premier League, FA Cup and UEFA Champions League.[9] In winning the UEFA Europa League under José Mourinho in 2016–17, they became one of five clubs to have won the original three main UEFA club competitions (the Champions League, Europa League and Cup Winners' Cup).

Management

Sir Jim Ratcliffe

Sir Jim Ratcliffe is a British billionaire and the founder of INEOS, one of the world's largest chemical companies. Known for his audacious business moves and passion for sports, Ratcliffe has built a reputation as a self-made industrialist with a keen eye for opportunity. From humble beginnings in Manchester, he rose to become one of the UK's wealthiest individuals, driven by a relentless pursuit of excellence and innovation. Beyond business, Ratcliffe's ventures span from owning football clubs to challenging the world's most extreme environments, embodying a spirit of adventure and ambition that inspires many.

Prospective Ownership Stake: Ratcliffe, through INEOS, expressed strong interest in acquiring a significant ownership stake in Manchester United, aiming to bring a fresh perspective and strategic leadership to the club.
Investment in Infrastructure: Ratcliffe is committed to investing in the club’s infrastructure, including upgrading Old Trafford and the training facilities, to maintain Manchester United's status as one of the world’s top football clubs.
Focus on Youth Development: He has emphasized the importance of nurturing young talent through Manchester United’s academy, ensuring a steady pipeline of future stars for the club.
Commitment to Success: Ratcliffe's involvement is driven by a passion for returning Manchester United to its glory days, focusing on both domestic and European success through strategic signings and strong leadership.
Long-Term Vision: With a deep understanding of sports and business, Ratcliffe aims to bring stability, sustainability, and long-term success to Manchester United, aligning the club's financial health with its competitive ambitions.

1. Erik ten Hag

Head Manager

Nationality: Dutch

Leadership & Vision: Erik ten Hag serves as the Head Manager of Manchester United, known for his tactical acumen and ability to develop cohesive teams. He brings a modern, attacking style of play to the club, focusing on high pressing and possession-based football.
Track Record: Prior to joining Manchester United, ten Hag achieved significant success with Ajax, where he led the team to domestic titles and a remarkable run in the UEFA Champions League, known for developing young talents.
Role:At United, his role involves setting the overall team strategy, making key tactical decisions, and driving the club towards winning major trophies. Ten Hag is also responsible for team selection, in-game management, and overseeing the development of players across all levels.

2. Ruud van Nistelrooy

Assistant Manager

Nationality: Dutch

Experience & Expertise: Ruud van Nistelrooy, a legendary former striker for Manchester United, brings his extensive experience and goal-scoring prowess to his role as Assistant Manager. Known for his clinical finishing and intelligence on the pitch, van Nistelrooy provides invaluable insights into attacking play.
Role: His primary role is to work closely with the forwards, focusing on improving their finishing, positioning, and overall attacking movements. Van Nistelrooy also assists ten Hag in tactical planning, particularly in offensive strategies and set-piece execution.

3. René Hake

Assistant Manager

Nationality: Dutch

Tactical Insight & Development: René Hake, with a strong background in coaching and tactical analysis, serves as another Assistant Manager at Manchester United. Hake is known for his detailed approach to match preparation and his ability to break down opponents' strategies.
Role: Hake's responsibilities include developing and implementing defensive strategies, overseeing the tactical training sessions, and assisting in the development of younger players. He plays a crucial role in match analysis, ensuring that the team is well-prepared for each game with specific tactical instructions.
